## The firm (fiction — invented for this workshop)

**Aura Build** is a boutique architecture firm specializing in **landmark civic and mixed-use developments that merge stadium-scale infrastructure with vertical urban districts.** They don't design buildings in isolation — they design the symbol a city builds itself around.

- **Founded:** 2018
- **Headquartered:** Singapore (with satellite studios in Dubai and Tokyo)
- **Team:** 42 architects, structural engineers, and urbanists
- **Tagline:** *We don't design buildings. We design how a city remembers itself.*

### Who they build for

- Tier-1 city governments planning identity-defining civic developments
- Sovereign wealth funds and master developers commissioning marquee assets
- Sports league authorities (FIFA, UEFA, IOC programme partners)
- Cultural institutions building generational landmarks

### What they build

- **Integrated arenas + sky-rise districts** (signature)
- Cultural and civic landmarks
- Master-planned waterfront precincts
- Mixed-use towers where the public realm is the brief

### Selected works (fictional, for the page)

- **AURELIA ARENA** — Marina Bay, Singapore (2026, 70,000 seats + 5-tower vertical district)
- **HELIX PAVILION** — Dubai Creek, UAE (2024, cultural pavilion + harbor reclamation)
- **MERIDIAN QUARTER** — Tokyo Bay, Japan (2025, 12-tower waterfront masterplan)
- **CAUSEWAY HOUSE** — Liverpool, UK (2023, civic + retail)

### Voice

Confident. Cinematic. Lightly poetic but technically grounded. Never corporate, never startup-y. Reads like an editorial monograph, not a sales page.

### Section 5 — Process

Three steps, plain prose, no icons.

**Brief into instinct.** We start every commission by spending two weeks not drawing. We listen to the city, the client, and the constraints until the building's reason to exist becomes obvious.

**Engineering as authorship.** Our structural engineers sit at the design table from day one. Geometry that isn't structurally honest doesn't make it past week three.

**Build with the city, not on it.** Every Aura project is a public-realm project first. The building is what the public realm is shaped around.

## Design guidelines (short — paste these into the builder verbatim)

**Aesthetic register:** Editorial luxury monograph. Closer to a Phaidon architecture book than a SaaS landing page. Closer to The Row than to Squarespace.

**Color**

- Canvas: deep ink black `#0E0F11` (NOT pure black)
- Primary type: warm bone white `#F1ECE2`
- Accent metal: brushed brass `#B8915E`
- Secondary accent (used once, sparingly): glass blue `#4A6B8A`
- Never use: pure white, pure black, gradients on text, drop shadows, neon

**Typography (mandatory — do NOT default to Inter or Söhne)**

- Display: a thin editorial serif — **GT Sectra Light**, **Tiempos Headline**, or **PP Editorial New** (pick one)
- Body: a clean grotesk — **Söhne Buch**, **Neue Haas Grotesk**, or **GT America** (pick one)
- All-caps wordmark and section labels: tight tracking, +40 letter-spacing, never bold
- Body text: 17–18px, line-height 1.6, max-width 65ch
- Display text: 6–10vw on hero typography, never below 48px

**Layout**

- Asymmetric. Editorial. The grid is a 12-column with frequent break-outs.
- Whitespace is the loudest element. Sections breathe with 160–240px vertical padding.
- Text is left-aligned with the occasional centered manifesto line. No justified text.
- No card shadows. No rounded corners above 4px. Edges and lines are crisp.

**Motion**

- Subtle only. The video carries the kinetic load.
- One choreographed entrance per section: text rises 24px and fades in over 800ms with 60ms stagger between lines.
- No parallax, no scroll-jacking, no scroll-snapping, no animated gradients, no Lottie illustrations.
- Hover states: cursor swap to a custom dot, image cards reveal a hairline outline, that's it.

**Imagery**

- The AURELIA ARENA hero video — looping, muted, autoplay, full-bleed.
- Project photos: single large photographic image per card, no overlay tints.
- No stock photography. No icons. No illustrations. No emoji.

**What this site is NOT**

- Not a SaaS landing page
- Not a portfolio template
- Not "modern minimal startup"
- Not Webflow-default
- Not a parallax storytelling experience
- Not a Behance entry trying to win an award

**What this site IS**

- A printed monograph in browser form
- One quiet, confident object you scroll through and remember
- The kind of site a sovereign-wealth-fund chairman screenshots and sends to his architect
