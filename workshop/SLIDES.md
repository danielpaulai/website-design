# Workshop Opening Deck — 15 Slides

A 15-slide deck for tonight's Aura Build workshop. Structured around a single story (the Ice Cube + Identity Votes from *Atomic Habits*) and a four-step system (Idea → Prompt → Video → Site).

Built to be pasted into **Gamma.app**, regenerated visually, and refined.

---

## How to use this with Gamma.app

1. Open **<https://gamma.app>** and click **Create new** → **Generate**
2. Choose **Paste in text** (not "Generate from prompt")
3. Copy the **Outline for Gamma** section below verbatim
4. Choose a **dark / luxury** theme — closest match: "Oasis", "Atlas", or "Marble" (dark variants)
5. Set image style to: **Editorial photography** or **Cinematic**
6. Generate. Then refine slide-by-slide using the **Detailed slide-by-slide** section below.

If Gamma offers an "outline-first" mode, use it — that gives you the most control before image generation kicks in.

## Visual style

| Token | Value |
|---|---|
| Canvas background | `#0E0F11` ink black |
| Type | `#F1ECE2` bone white |
| Accent | `#B8915E` brushed brass |
| Secondary accent | `#4A6B8A` glass blue |
| Display font | **Fraunces** (light, italic for emphasis) |
| Body font | **Manrope** (regular, 500 for emphasis) |
| Aspect ratio | 16:9 |

**Aesthetic anchor:** A Phaidon architecture monograph crossed with a film title sequence. Closer to The Row than to a SaaS deck. Big editorial type. Generous whitespace. Hairline dividers. Cinematic photography. Zero icons, zero gradients, zero stock-photo people in headsets.

## Outline for Gamma (paste-ready)

```
1. Aura Build · Workshop
Subtitle: From idea to live website. Tonight.

5. What we are building tonight
Aura Build — architecture for the way a city remembers itself. A fictional Singapore architecture firm with a real working website, real video, real URL.

6. Why this matters now
Three years ago: $50,000 and a team of twelve. Tonight: $5 of credits and 90 minutes. Architectural visualization plus web design plus deployment — the whole stack collapsed into prompts.

7. The four-part system
Idea to prompt with Claude Code. Prompt to video with KIE.AI. Video to website with Frontend Design skill. Website to live with GitHub and Vercel. Forty minutes of work. Ninety minutes of session. The rest is breathing room and fixing things together.

8. Live preview
Watch this for ten seconds. Aurelia Arena, a fictional Singapore stadium. Blueprint to real building. This is what you will be making.

9. Step 1 — Idea to prompt
You give Claude Code a vague three-sentence vision. The Seedance skill returns a 200-word cinematographer-grade prompt with specific lens millimeters, film stocks, and loop topology. You don't need to know what anamorphic flare means. The skill knows.

10. Step 2 — Prompt to video
Paste prompt into KIE.AI. About thirty cents per render. About ninety seconds wait. You get a 1080p cinema-grade clip. Same render commercially: five thousand dollars.

11. Step 3 — Video to website
Claude Code plus Anthropic's Frontend Design skill builds the site. Hero is your video, looping, no overlay. Five sections of editorial copy. Real responsive Next.js with TypeScript and Tailwind. Not a Squarespace template — a Phaidon-grade single-page object.

12. Step 4 — Site to live
Push to GitHub. Deploy to Vercel. Three CLI commands. Ninety seconds. You have a URL ending in vercel.app. Buy a real domain in thirty seconds and point it. Now you have your-name.com.

13. What you walk away with
A live URL on the public internet. Your own AI-rendered cinematic video. A skill ninety-nine percent of agencies cannot yet do. The identity of someone who ships. Proof you can text your mother. Atomic Habits says every action is a vote for the person you want to become. Tonight you are casting a big one.

14. The room rules
Lean in. When something works, name it. When you are stuck, ask immediately. We move at the speed of the room. No silent confusion — raise a hand the moment you are lost. Steal everything — code, prompts, copy, screenshots. We are not student and teacher tonight. We are a small studio building together.

15. Let's go
The ice melts at thirty-two degrees. Open Claude Code. We start now.
```

### Slide 3 / The Story — The Ice Cube

**On-slide:**
> AN ICE CUBE IN A COLD ROOM.
> *— James Clear, Atomic Habits*

**Speaker (90 sec — slow, story rhythm):**
"Imagine an ice cube sitting on a table. The room is twenty-five degrees Fahrenheit. You want to melt the ice cube, so you start heating the room. Twenty-six. Nothing. Twenty-seven. Nothing. Twenty-eight. Nothing visible. Twenty-nine. Thirty. Thirty-one. Still no change.

You're working hard. The temperature is rising. The ice cube hasn't moved.

Then at thirty-two degrees... the ice cube melts.

What happened? Did one single degree do all the work?

No. Every degree of warming was doing the work. We just couldn't see it. The ice was moving toward its phase change the entire time. Thirty-two degrees was simply when invisible work became visible result.

This is true of skills. This is true of careers. And this is true of you, sitting in this room right now."

**Image prompt:**
> Macro photograph of a single ice cube on a black slate surface, beginning to melt, one suspended water droplet caught mid-fall, soft side light from a window at 4500K, deep dark background, ARRI Alexa look, shallow depth of field.

**Design hint:** Quote in `Fraunces` italic, large (50pt+). Light-on-dark. A single hairline rule in `#4A6B8A` glass blue under the quote.

## Bonus — Speaker preparation tips

- **Read the deck out loud once** before the session. The cadence is built around the story rhythm. The Ice Cube slide especially — slow it down.
- **The room rules slide is when energy can sag.** Move quickly. Don't preach.
- **Slide 13 is your identity-shift moment.** Slow down. Make eye contact. Let the silence between sentences land.
- **Slide 15 is your transition into work mode.** Say the line, then literally turn to your screen and open Claude Code. The room will follow.

## What the deck is NOT

- It's not a curriculum doc. The platform-specific guides ([MAC-GUIDE.md](MAC-GUIDE.md) and [WINDOWS-GUIDE.md](WINDOWS-GUIDE.md)) handle the install. This deck is opening + framing only.
- It's not a sales deck. The "money" angle is woven in (slides 2 and 6) but the primary frame is identity — making them feel like the kind of person who ships, not selling them on a course.
- It's not bullet-heavy. Each slide has one big idea. Big serif. Big space.
